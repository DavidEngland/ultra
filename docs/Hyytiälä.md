Hyytiälä: a forest research station in Finland

Hyytiälä Forestry Field Station, located in Juupajoki, hosts SMEAR II (Station for Measuring Ecosystem-Atmosphere Relations), Finland's second SMEAR station. The site is a long-running, instrumented boreal forest observatory and is widely used in atmospheric and ecosystem science.

SMEAR II includes a tall measurement mast and a dense network of in-forest and soil instruments. Continuous observations typically include greenhouse gases, aerosol properties, meteorology, radiation, turbulence, and ecosystem exchange processes. This multi-decadal record supports studies of biosphere-atmosphere coupling, air quality, and climate feedbacks.

The surrounding stand is a managed Scots pine forest, often used as a reference environment for boreal process studies and method development. Because measurements are integrated across canopy, understory, soil, and atmospheric layers, Hyytiälä serves as a key benchmark site for testing and comparing models, including spectral and DCT-based approaches.
